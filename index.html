About Me

Who am I?

Hi, I'm Danielle. I am a scrum product owner and UX expert specializing in digital products and e-commerce.

I have five years' experience in technical startups

Being a millennial and coming from a non-technical background in a highly competitive landscape, I hope to help and inspire others who want to gain technical expertise but are unsure where to start.

How am I different?

My greatest asset is my ability to enthusiastically take on any problem to produce impactful results.

I champion being the voice of the end user, through leveraging Jobs Theory, I use qualitative and quantitative analysis to determine the drivers of user behavior which allows me to identify opportuntities for product enhancements that benefit our users most

I enjoy throwing myself into the unknown, the messiest of problems, making sense and order of it to optimize and drive business growth. Through several years in various tech start ups I've gained multi-faceted experience in different roles from UX and design, to data analysis, to engineer management and acting as scrum master, to leadership and human resources.

I have learned how to deal with investors, weighing out the benefits of third party vendors, finding developers and general talent in a competitive landscape with scarce funding, and working cross functionally with growing departments to drive company culture and efficient business processes.

This site is to share both my experiences for upcoming and current product managers, for anyone who wants to break into a technical role, and for those who are looking for product management consulting services for their own company.

What are my accomplishments?

  • Problem: Development was slow and disorganized, deadlines were never hit and scope creep was unmanageable.
    Solution: Implementing an agile engineering approach and Gantt chart roadmap with defined KPIs
    Results: Increased company productivity and engineering velocity 300% and $5,000 overall net revenue in preparation for Series A fundraising round
  • Problem: 60% of inventory in our database was not listed for sale on site because we could not identify the UPC.
    Solution: Analyzed all unclassified inventory items and identified ways to improve our algorithm (ie. increasing the threshold of the Levenshtein distance algorithm, creating a fuzzy match off of name and other item attributes, mapping most common shorthand terms to its complete word), coupled with building an external facing tool which replaced all free text inputs with pre-determined drop downs to standardize the way inventory was being inputted.
    Results: Created an additional $50K in monthly recurring revenue and ~10K additional purchasable products per month for consumers on site.
  • Problem: Order substitutions accounted for 20% of customer successes calls. At our rate of growth and substitution requests, we needed to hire 2-3 additional customer success representatives to manage the volume.
    Solution: Built an external facing operational tool allowing suppliers to completely self-service the substitution process and notify customers, cutting out customer service’s role in the process
    Results: Saved over $200K in employment expenses.
  • Problem: Credit recovery team could only leverage email to demand credit payments resulting in 60% in unrecovered credits.
    Solution: Built an external facing tool to manage the Credit Recovery process; bringing the process into a system they used daily for order processing, automating notifications to our clients, a disputing process, and credit retrieval from their account.
    Results: Recovered over $1MM in losses in the first quarter post-launch.

Phase Gate Process

I like to adapt this proces to agile for how I convey the phases of a project to the business-side. We define projects we discuss in our stakeholder meetings by these phases, each phase denotes the action to be taken in the meeting. For example, items in Phase 0 mean the discussion is focused around cost benefits, risks, market-share, and competittion and the guiding question is: Should we do this project?

Agile Manifesto

Open Web Application Security Project (OWASP)

The Open Web Application Security Project (OWASP) is a 501(c)(3) worldwide not-for-profit charitable organization focused on improving the security of software. As a product manager, you will likely be writing features that has security impllications and it's important that you're aware and across best practices for this.
